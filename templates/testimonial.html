Kalyan Prusty-

IISc, Bangalore
Graduation Year: 2020

Robotics is one field that is very close to my heart, and even today, though I am occupied at work, I try to steal around 4-6 hours every week from working on some cool robots :) In my opinion, to get started in robotics, there is no better place than your local club. I started my robotics journey at MNNIT itself, where we tried to understand and carry forward the projects and our seniors' initiatives and pass the baton to the new budding engineers. They, in my opinion, have taken the club to new heights and have worked extremely hard to make the MNNIT Robotics club a world-class one. Robotics is a field that gives you the perspective to think from the system engineering point of view and look beyond the narrow area of expertise that one has in a given domain. Opportunities ranging from the buzzwords of AI/ML to Computer Vision, Estimation, Control and Planning, Simulation, Circuit Designing, Sensor designing and planning, Power management, actuators design, locomotion engineering, etc. etc. are some of the few fields to name that one can work on. Additionally, you meet some great people and get to exchange ideas. In fact, today, a lot of entrepreneurs are working in the field of robotics. And finally, Robotics is the future. From Mars/Space exploration to the deepest point in the Mariana Trench, the boundaries that we know have been reached by robots only. From the day-to-day task of household chores to the most complex ones of understanding and simulating emotions or medical surgery, Robotics has a role to play. The application of robotics is still something that the world is discovering every other day.
